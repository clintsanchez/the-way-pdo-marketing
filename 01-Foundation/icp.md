# The Way Church PDO and Preschool — Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

## Primary Persona: Faith-Focused Young Parent

### Demographics

**Age:** 28-42 years old  
**Gender:** Primarily female (decision-maker), but household-based decision  
**Location:** Denham Springs, LA (primary) + 5-mile radius  
**Household Income:** $60,000 - $150,000+  
**Education:** High school to college-educated  
**Marital Status:** Married or in long-term partnership  
**Number of Children:** 1-3 (planning for or actively raising young children)

### Psychographics

**Faith & Values:**
- Active in church/faith community
- Christian values central to parenting approach
- Seeks to raise children with strong moral foundation
- Regular church attendance (weekly+)
- Values community aligned with their beliefs

**Parenting Philosophy:**
- Believes quality early education matters
- Wants balanced approach (academics + character/values)
- Seeks loving, safe environment over institutional care
- Values personal relationships with teachers
- Wants visible progress/communication

**Lifestyle:**
- Actively involved in children's activities & education
- Community-oriented (church, school, local groups)
- Conscious of what influences their children
- Budget-conscious but willing to invest in quality
- Limited time (working family, juggling schedules)

**Media Consumption:**
- Primary: Facebook (main source of parenting info, community connection)
- Secondary: Google search (looking for local options)
- Tertiary: Word-of-mouth, referrals, church community
- Less active on: Instagram, TikTok (though may use)

## Pain Points & Concerns

### Top 3 Pain Points

**1. Finding Quality Care That Matches Values**
- Frustration with secular-only programs
- Concern that faith won't be respected/included
- Difficulty assessing actual curriculum and teaching approach
- Fear of compromising values to find "good" preschool

**2. Safety, Security & Trust**
- Anxiety about leaving child in someone else's care
- Need for thorough background checks & vetting
- Desire for transparent communication & frequent updates
- Concern about class size and teacher-to-student ratios
- "Will my child be safe and happy?"

**3. Balancing Work, Family & Faith**
- Time constraints (working parents, multiple kids)
- Need for flexible scheduling or extended hours
- Exhaustion from juggling everything
- Guilt about not being with child during day
- Want peace of mind while working

### Secondary Concerns

- **Affordability** — Quality care is expensive; seeking good value
- **Academic Readiness** — Want child prepared for kindergarten
- **Communication** — Need regular updates, photos, progress reports
- **Flexibility** — Prefer schedule options (full-time, part-time, seasonal)
- **Community** — Value connection with other families
- **Practical Logistics** — Drop-off/pick-up times, meal plans, activity options

## Buying Behavior & Decision Process

### Decision Timeline

**Awareness Phase (3-6 months before):**
- Starts thinking about preschool needs
- Asks friends/family for recommendations
- Begins searching online
- May visit multiple programs

**Consideration Phase (2-3 months before):**
- Compares 3-5 options actively
- Reads reviews and testimonials
- May visit campuses
- Talks to other parents
- Evaluates fit, values, cost

**Decision Phase (Final month):**
- Makes final visit/meeting
- Enrollment paperwork
- Confirms commitment
- Registers child

**Duration Typical:** 2-4 months from awareness to enrollment

### Key Objections to Overcome

1. **"I want my child with me, not in preschool"**
   - Solution: Emphasize partnership, quality time, specific benefits

2. **"How do I know they'll be safe?"**
   - Solution: Transparent policies, staff credentials, security measures, testimonials

3. **"Will my faith values be respected?"**
   - Solution: Show Christian integration clearly, staff alignment, curriculum details

4. **"It's too expensive"**
   - Solution: Value proposition, quality justification, maybe flexible options

5. **"There are other options in town"**
   - Solution: Clear differentiation, unique positioning, testimonials

6. **"I'm not ready to decide yet"**
   - Solution: Email nurture, relationship-building, stay in touch

## Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)

**Average Enrollment Duration:**
- **Minimum:** 1 year (single age group)
- **Average:** 2-3 years (infant through Pre-K)
- **Extended:** 4+ years (multiple children, referrals)

**Revenue Per Customer:**
- Monthly tuition (example): $1,000-1,500
- Annual per child: ~$12,000-18,000
- 2-year CLV: ~$24,000-36,000
- 3-year CLV: ~$36,000-54,000

**High-Value Segment:**
- Families with multiple children → 2-3+ enrollments
- Strong referrers → Additional families attracted
- Community advocates → Word-of-mouth growth

## Seasonal Behavior

### Fall Enrollment Push (July-August)
**Pain Point:** Back-to-school planning, preschool decisions urgent  
**Messaging:** "Start your child's journey," "Limited spots available," "Summer to fall transition"  
**Channels:** Google Ads, Facebook, Local search

### Spring Inquiry Period (February-April)
**Pain Point:** Planning for summer/fall ahead  
**Messaging:** "Plan ahead," "Summer camp," "Fall enrollment opening"  
**Channels:** Email, Facebook, Referral requests

### Year-Round Community (Sept-May)
**Pain Point:** Regular parent engagement, retention  
**Messaging:** Community, updates, progress, events  
**Channels:** Email, Facebook, In-person events

### Summer Programs (June-July)
**Pain Point:** Summer childcare gap  
**Messaging:** "Fun, faith-filled activities," "VBS," "Summer camp"  
**Channels:** Email, Facebook, Church networks

## How to Find Them

### Acquisition Channels (Highest ROI)

| Channel | Audience | Best For |
|---------|----------|----------|
| **Facebook Ads** | Local parents (Denham Springs) | Fall enrollment push, VBS, Summer camp |
| **Google Local/Maps** | Searching for preschool options | Year-round (especially enrollment seasons) |
| **Referral/Word-of-Mouth** | Existing parent networks | Highest-value leads, low CAC |
| **Church Networks** | Faith community | Targeted, aligned audience |
| **Email Nurture** | Inquiry list | Conversion of interested prospects |
| **Local Community** | NextDoor, local FB groups | Very local, high relevance |

### Recommended Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) Target
- **Organic (Referral/SEO):** $0-200 per enrollment
- **Email (nurture existing):** $50-150 per enrollment
- **Paid Ads (Google/Facebook):** $200-500 per enrollment
