# The Way Church PDO and Preschool — Brand Voice & Tone Guide

## Brand Personality

**Core Adjectives:**
- **Warm** — Genuine, welcoming, approachable, caring
- **Trustworthy** — Reliable, honest, consistent, dependable
- **Nurturing** — Supportive, growth-focused, patient, encouraging
- **Faith-Centered** — Values-driven, spiritual, purposeful, community-oriented
- **Professional** — Competent, organized, quality-focused, established

### What Each Means in Practice

**Warm:** We speak like we're talking to a friend, not an institution. We smile through our words. We celebrate milestones with families. We're accessible and human.

**Trustworthy:** We follow through on promises. We're transparent about programs, safety, and policies. Parents can rely on us completely. We've earned credentials and experience.

**Nurturing:** We see each child as unique. We support development across all areas (academic, emotional, spiritual, social). We partner with parents, not dictate to them.

**Faith-Centered:** Our Christian values aren't hidden — they're central. We talk about God's love, spiritual growth, and Christian community. It's not generic spirituality; it's specifically Christian.

**Professional:** We respect parents' time and concerns. We're organized and responsive. We maintain high standards for safety, curriculum, and care.

## Tone Spectrum

### Position: Warm Professional + Faith-Centered

**Formal ←→ Casual**  
**Position: 60% Formal / 40% Casual**

We're more professional than casual, but not stuffy. Think of it as "professional but personal."

**Examples:**

❌ **Too Formal (Too Corporate):**
"Our institution provides comprehensive early childhood educational services aligned with Christian pedagogical frameworks."

✅ **Right Balance:**
"We provide quality preschool programs where your child grows academically, emotionally, and spiritually."

❌ **Too Casual (Unprofessional):**
"ur kids r gonna luv us lol 🎉"

---

### Corporate ←→ Personal

**Position: Personal + Community-Focused**

We talk about "we" and "our families," not "the program" and "students."

**Examples:**

❌ **Too Corporate:**
"The Way PDO's child development curriculum incorporates faith-based educational methodologies."

✅ **Right Balance:**
"We grow their hearts AND their minds through Christian values and quality learning."

❌ **Too Personal (Loses credibility):**
"My kids love it SO much and we're obsessed with it!!"

## Voice Dos

### Do's — What We Embrace

- ✓ **Use simple, clear language** — Avoid jargon. Say "safe, loving environment" not "pedagogically secure space"
- ✓ **Lead with parents' concerns** — Safety, growth, faith values, flexibility
- ✓ **Talk about outcomes** — "Your child will grow spiritually and academically"
- ✓ **Use "we" and "our"** — "We partner with families" vs. "The Way PDO provides services"
- ✓ **Include faith naturally** — "We teach Christian values" not "We integrate faith elements"
- ✓ **Emphasize partnership** — "We work with you" not "We babysit while you work"
- ✓ **Use warm, active language** — "Discover," "Grow," "Thrive," "Experience," "Connect"
- ✓ **Include specific details** — "Prayer at snack time" vs. "Spiritual development activities"
- ✓ **Show parent benefits too** — "Peace of mind knowing they're in safe hands"
- ✓ **Tell stories** — Child milestones, family testimonials, program highlights

### Sentence Style

- **Conversational** — Write like you're explaining to another parent
- **Short paragraphs** — Easier to scan, especially on mobile
- **Active voice** — "We nurture hearts" not "Hearts are nurtured"
- **Specific examples** — "Bible stories, songs, and hands-on activities" not "faith-based curriculum"

---

## Voice Don'ts

### Don'ts — What We Avoid

- ✗ **Never be condescending** — Don't talk down to parents or children
- ✗ **Avoid excessive jargon** — "Pedagogical," "developmental domains," "learning outcomes" (use plain language)
- ✗ **Don't be overly salesy** — We're here to serve, not manipulate
- ✗ **Never minimize faith** — Don't make it seem optional or secondary
- ✗ **Avoid corporate speak** — "Leverage," "synergy," "best practices," "stakeholders"
- ✗ **Don't be generic** — Avoid language every preschool uses ("quality education," "safe environment")
- ✗ **Never sound impersonal** — "Students," "the program," "our services" (too institutional)
- ✗ **Avoid negativity** — Don't criticize competitors or other parenting styles
- ✗ **Don't be preachy** — Share faith values, don't preach at families
- ✗ **Avoid dated language** — "Daycare," "childcare provider" (we're a preschool program)

### Words & Phrases to Ban

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| "Childcare" | "Preschool," "early education," "program" |
| "Daycare" | "PDO," "preschool," "faith-based program" |
| "Babysitting" | "Care," "nurturing," "supporting growth" |
| "Students" | "Children," "kids," "young learners" |
| "Clients" | "Families," "parents," "our community" |
| "Curriculum materials" | "Learning experiences," "activities" |
| "Behavior management" | "Guidance," "teaching," "helping them grow" |
| "The Way PDO" (every time) | "We" (more personal) |
| "Synergy," "leverage," "best practices" | Simpler, clearer words |
| Generic "quality" | Specific (e.g., "trained teachers," "small class sizes") |

## What We Sound Like

### Example 1: Homepage Hero Copy
❌ **Wrong:**
"The Way PDO provides comprehensive early childhood education services in a faith-based environment."

✅ **Right:**
"We nurture hearts and minds. Through prayer, Bible stories, songs, and hands-on learning, we help your child grow spiritually AND academically — in a safe, loving community."

---

### Example 2: Program Description
❌ **Wrong:**
"Our 3-year-old program incorporates developmental psychology principles within Christian educational frameworks."

✅ **Right:**
"In our 3-year-old class, your child learns through play, discovery, and guided activities. We focus on social skills, pre-academic learning, and building a foundation for faith. Class size is small, teachers are trained, and every day includes prayer and Bible lessons."

---

### Example 3: Email to Prospective Parents
❌ **Wrong:**
"We appreciate your inquiry regarding our preschool services. Please complete the attached form to schedule an assessment and program orientation."

✅ **Right:**
"We'd love to meet you and your little one! Every family is unique, and we want to learn about your child and what matters most to you. Let's schedule a time to visit — you can see our classrooms, meet our teachers, and ask any questions. Call us at (225) 369-2789 or let me know what works best for your schedule."

---

### Example 4: Social Media Post
❌ **Wrong:**
"The Way PDO is committed to providing quality early childhood education services aligned with Christian values."

✅ **Right:**
"Nothing like watching a 2-year-old's eyes light up when they learn about Jesus's love ❤️ That's what we do every day — grow happy, healthy kids in our faith-filled community. Spots open for Fall enrollment! 🎉"

## Red Flags — When We're Off-Brand

If you see any of these, it's time to revise:

- 🚩 Using "students" or "clients" instead of "children" or "families"
- 🚩 Being overly corporate or robotic in tone
- 🚩 Minimizing faith or making it seem optional
- 🚩 Comparing unfavorably to other programs
- 🚩 Using outdated terms ("daycare," "babysitting")
- 🚩 Including unexplained jargon
- 🚩 Losing the personal, warm tone
- 🚩 Sounding like every other preschool
- 🚩 Being preachy or judgmental about other parenting choices
- 🚩 Using generic stock phrases

## Checklist: Is This On-Brand?

Before hitting send/publish, ask:

- [ ] Would a parent understand this without needing to ask questions?
- [ ] Does it sound like we're talking TO them, not AT them?
- [ ] Is faith represented naturally and prominently?
- [ ] Does it highlight specific benefits, not generic claims?
- [ ] Is it warm and approachable while still professional?
- [ ] Would we say it in person?
- [ ] Does it reflect our actual practices and values?
- [ ] Is it compelling (would it make a parent interested)?
